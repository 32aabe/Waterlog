# Waterlog Screen Specification

# 01. Map (Relationship Landscape)

---

## Purpose

The Map is the emotional home of Waterlog.

Although users see it as a map, internally it should always be treated as a **Relationship Landscape** rather than a traditional map.

Its purpose is not navigation.

Its purpose is not bird discovery.

Its purpose is to reveal the quiet relationships the user has built with places where life gathers around water.

The user should immediately feel:

> "This is my landscape."

> "These places have become part of my life."

---

# Core Philosophy

Waterlog visualizes relationships, not records.

A Spot is not meaningful because it contains many observations.

A Spot becomes meaningful because a relationship has quietly deepened over time.

Relationships deepen through:

- returning naturally
- witnessing seasonal change
- noticing life again and again
- remembering the place

The Map should never communicate progress.

It should communicate familiarity.

---

# Emotional Goal

Opening the Map should feel like opening a rain-softened field notebook.

The user should experience:

- calm
- quiet wonder
- curiosity
- attachment

Never:

- urgency
- productivity
- completion
- competition

The interface should invite observation rather than action.

---

# Primary Question

The Map exists to answer only one question:

> **"Where have I built relationships?"**

Every design decision should reinforce this question.

---

# Information Hierarchy

## Primary

Relationship Marks (Spots)

## Secondary

Current location

Capture button

## Hidden Until Needed

Search

Filters

Settings

Export

Statistics

---

# Relationship Marks (Spots)

Spots are not map pins.

They are gentle traces left by meaningful encounters.

A first observation appears as:

- quiet
- faint
- almost disappearing

As the relationship deepens:

- the mark becomes richer
- edges become softer
- presence becomes calmer
- subtle water-like ripples may appear
- the place feels settled rather than enlarged

The Spot should never simply become bigger.

It should feel more alive.

---

# Relationship Depth

Relationship depth should never be calculated from visit count alone.

Instead it gradually emerges from:

- meaningful repeated visits
- observing life across time
- returning through different seasons
- continued attention
- memories accumulating

The exact algorithm should remain intentionally invisible.

Users should never feel they are optimizing a score.

They should simply feel that some places have quietly become part of their lives.

---

# Interaction

## Tap

Open the Spot.

The transition should feel like gently diving beneath the surface of water.

Never like opening another page.

---

## Long Press (Optional)

Show a small memory preview.

No editing.

No utility shortcuts.

No management actions.

---

# Motion

Movement should borrow from water.

- slow easing
- soft fades
- gentle expansion
- subtle breathing

Avoid:

- bounce
- snap
- celebration animations
- exaggerated transitions

Motion should reduce noise rather than attract attention.

---

# Visual Language

The Map should feel handcrafted.

Avoid:

- utility map aesthetics
- bright colors
- heat maps
- dashboard layouts
- aggressive markers

Instead use:

- muted natural colors
- generous whitespace
- organic forms
- soft textures
- quiet visual rhythm

The interface should resemble a personal landscape rather than a navigation tool.

---

# AI Behavior

AI is almost invisible.

It never tells users where to go.

It never recommends hotspots.

It never ranks places.

It never explains the Map.

Instead, AI quietly helps users notice relationships they may have overlooked.

If AI appears on the Map, it should feel like a tiny handwritten note in the margin of a notebook.

Never like a chatbot.

Never like an assistant.

---

# Things to Avoid

Never include:

- Levels
- XP
- Achievements
- Badges
- Streaks
- Leaderboards
- Rare bird rankings
- Popularity indicators
- Heat maps
- Utility-first navigation
- "Top Places"
- Completion percentages

Nothing should encourage optimization.

Everything should encourage attention.

---

# Success Criteria

A successful Map makes the user think:

> "I remember this place."

rather than

> "I have many records."

Later, after quietly looking around the landscape:

> "Maybe I'll stop by there again."

If users naturally return to a place because of the relationship they feel—not because of a reminder or reward—the Map has succeeded.

---

# Design Principle

The Map is not a map of where life exists.

It is a landscape of places where life has become meaningful to the user.

The goal of the interface is not to help users discover more places.

It is to help them see the places they already care about more deeply.

Waterlog does not celebrate discovery.

Waterlog celebrates returning.

## Capture Transition

Capture must be reachable within 0.5 seconds.

The primary interaction is a simple tap.

Optional interactions such as dragging may enrich the experience but must never replace the fastest path.

Animations should accompany navigation, not delay it.
