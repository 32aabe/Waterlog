# Spot Screen

## Purpose

The Spot screen is the emotional center of Waterlog.

It is where a place becomes memorable.

A Spot should be understood before it is analyzed.

Users should first *feel* the place,
then choose whether to read its history.

The Spot screen therefore consists of two separate layers.

---

# Layer 1 — Place Portrait

Layer 1 is not a dashboard.

It is not a summary.

It is a portrait of the place.

The goal is that someone can understand the place within a few seconds without reading much text.

This layer should remain almost entirely static.

It should never become a long scrolling page.

## Structure

Top

- Place name
- Quiet status (Alive, Dry, Frozen...)
- Since...

Middle

- Large representative illustration or photograph

Between the title and image, only minimal supporting information may appear.

Examples:

- water state icon
- bird activity icon

Never large cards or dashboard-like components.

Below the image

One short sentence describing the character of the place.

Example:

"A quiet puddle where sparrows often stop after rain."

One action only:

View Story

Nothing else.

---

# Illustration Philosophy

Eventually this screen will not rely only on photos.

Instead, it should support a reusable illustration system.

The illustration is built from modular parts.

Examples:

Water type

- puddle
- stream
- river
- marsh
- fountain

Water condition

- dry
- shallow
- full
- frozen
- after rain

Bird activity

- none
- low
- medium
- high

These are visual indicators.

They are not scientific counts.

Bird numbers are symbolic.

The purpose is helping users immediately understand the atmosphere of the place.

---

# Layer 2 — Story

Story is entered explicitly from Layer 1.

This layer contains:

- chronological moments
- photos
- notes
- bird observations

This is where reading happens.

Layer 2 may scroll.

Layer 1 should not.

---

# Design Principles

Layer 1 answers:

"What kind of place is this?"

Layer 2 answers:

"What has happened here?"

Never mix these two purposes.

The place comes before the records.

Feeling comes before information.
