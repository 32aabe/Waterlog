# Visual Direction

This document defines the visual language of Waterlog.

Unlike the Brand Book or Design Manifesto, this document focuses on how the product should feel visually.

Whenever future UI, UX, animation, or interaction decisions are made, this document should be considered alongside the Design Manifesto.

If there is any conflict, philosophy always comes before visuals.

---

# Overall Feeling

Waterlog should never feel like productivity software.

It should feel like opening a quiet field notebook after walking through the rain.

The interface should disappear behind places, memories, and photographs.

The product should feel:

- calm
- immersive
- tactile
- cinematic
- natural
- emotionally warm
- reflective

---

# Visual Principles

## Photography comes first

Large photographs are the primary visual element.

The interface exists to support the user's memories, not compete with them.

---

## Quiet hierarchy

Navigation should stay subtle.

Content should always receive the most attention.

---

## Soft depth

Avoid hard borders and rigid cards.

Layers should feel soft, lightweight, and natural.

---

## Organic spacing

Layouts should breathe.

Generous spacing is preferred over dense information.

---

## Motion like water

Animations should settle rather than snap.

Motion should feel calm, fluid, and almost invisible.

---

## Natural materials

Visual inspiration comes from:

- rain
- paper
- stone
- water
- soft light
- seasons
- field notebooks

Never imitate these literally.

Instead, let them influence the emotional atmosphere.
