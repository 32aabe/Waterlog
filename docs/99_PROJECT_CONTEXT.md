# What is Waterlog?

Waterlog began as a research project about bird-water interactions in urban environments.

During that research, the project gradually changed.

The focus shifted away from birds themselves and toward the places that support life.

Waterlog is no longer simply a bird recording application.

It is a place-centered observation journal that helps people build lasting relationships with places where life gathers around water.

The long-term goal is to support meaningful ecological observation while naturally generating valuable citizen science data.

---

# Core Philosophy

Waterlog is not designed to maximize app usage.

It is designed to help people notice the real world.

The best outcome is not that users spend more time inside the app.

The best outcome is that they begin noticing water and life without needing the app at all.

---

# What Waterlog is NOT

Waterlog is NOT:

- an achievement system
- a social network
- a hotspot ranking app
- a competitive citizen science platform
- a dashboard for collecting statistics
- an AI-first product

Birds are important.

But places come first.

Data is valuable.

But attachment comes first.

---

# Design Direction

The interface should feel:

- quiet
- calm
- natural
- reflective
- field-note like

Avoid:

- bright gamification
- urgency
- flashy animations
- reward systems
- excessive notifications

Animation should feel like water.

Motion should support attention, not demand it.

---

# AI Philosophy

AI is a quiet companion.

Its purpose is to reduce effort, not curiosity.

Capture should remain extremely fast.

AI becomes more active only during reflection.

AI may:

- identify birds
- infer weather
- summarize observations
- detect repeated places
- generate Spot Stories

AI should never replace observation.

---

# Spot Philosophy

A Spot is not a GPS point.

A Spot begins when a person notices a place.

Repeated visits deepen the relationship.

Repeated visits by wildlife deepen the meaning.

The goal is not collecting Spots.

The goal is developing attachment.

---

# Relationship Philosophy

A Spot is not valuable because it contains many records.

A Spot becomes meaningful when a relationship deepens.

That relationship grows through:

- repeated visits by the user
- repeated appearances of life
- changes across seasons
- water returning and disappearing over time

Waterlog does not visualize progress.

It visualizes deepening relationships.

---

# Time Philosophy

Meaning comes from change.

One observation has little meaning.

Patterns across weeks, seasons, and years create meaning.

Waterlog always prefers showing change over showing totals.

---

# Current Development Goal

Current priority is the AIR project demo.

Focus on:

- Capture
- Map
- Spot Story
- Journal

Everything else is secondary.

Do not introduce unnecessary complexity.

Finish a coherent experience before adding new features.

---

# Design Principle

Whenever making a design decision, ask:

Does this make people notice places more?

Does this encourage people to return?

Does this help build a relationship with a place?

Does this quietly guide people back into the real world?

If the answer is no,

do not build it.
